Abstract

Existing attention accelerators often trade exact softmax semantics or suffer from sequential depth that limits FP32 throughput on long sequences[cite: 1717]. We present ELSA, which preserves standard softmax semantics in real arithmetic [cite: 1718] and casts the online softmax update into a prefix scan over an associative monoid of triples $(m, S, W)$[cite: 1719]. ELSA is Tensor-Core independent [cite: 1720] and serves as a drop-in replacement with no retraining required[cite: 1722]. It achieves $O(n)$ extra memory and $O(\log n)$ parallel depth [cite: 1719], improving FP32 inference latency over memory-efficient SDPA by 1.3–3.5x[cite: 1722, 1753].

Method: Monoid Reformulation

The core of ELSA is reformulating the inherently sequential online softmax recurrence into a parallelizable prefix scan[cite: 1832, 1834]. We define the attention state as a triple $(m, S, W)$ where $m$ is the running maximum, $S$ is the normalized sum, and $W$ is the weighted sum[cite: 1792, 1805].

We prove these states form an associative monoid under the merge operator $\oplus$[cite: 1750, 1833]: $$u_a \oplus u_b = \text{renorm}(\max(m_a, m_b), \text{un}(u_a) + \text{un}(u_b))$$ This allows us to implement a two-level parallel scan: intra-block Hillis-Steele and inter-block Blelloch[cite: 1721, 1823, 1854].

Experimental Benchmarks

ELSA was evaluated on NVIDIA A100 (40GB) and Jetson TX2 across various vision and language tasks[cite: 1889, 1897].

  • FP32 Latency: ELSA achieves 1.3x speedup at 1K tokens, 3.1x at 4K, and 3.5x at 16K tokens vs. memory-efficient SDPA[cite: 1736, 1753, 2004, 2005].
  • Memory Efficiency: Uses significantly lower peak memory in FP16 compared to FlashAttention-2/3 at 16K tokens (0.19 GB vs. 0.29/0.36 GB)[cite: 1754].
  • Exactness: Verified via drift metrics showing near-machine-precision agreement ($||\delta P||_{\infty} \approx 3.12 \times 10^{-17}$)[cite: 2290, 2291].

Real-World Deployment

ELSA is a drop-in replacement for foundation models including CLIP, SAM, and ViT[cite: 1751, 1897].

  • Foundation Models: Achieves up to 2.15x speedup and 39.6% memory reduction for CLIP ViT-L/14 at high resolutions (560px)[cite: 2361, 2364].
  • 3D Vision: In Visual Geometry-Grounded Transformer (VGGT), ELSA-FP32 yields a 2.34x speedup over xFormers at 150 frames[cite: 2381, 2393].
  • Embedded Systems: On Jetson TX2, ELSA consistently reduces latency by 1.5–2.0x relative to PyTorch Math kernels[cite: 2117, 2119].
