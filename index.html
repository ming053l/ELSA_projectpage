TL;DR

Standard attention is slow, and FlashAttention (FA) is strictly bound to specialized hardware (Tensor Cores). ELSA breaks this limitation by turning attention into a "Parallel Scan" problem. It achieves O(log n) parallel depth and O(n) memory on any hardware—from data-center GPUs to embedded devices—without losing precision or requiring zero retraining. Drop it into CLIP or SAM and get up to 3.5x speedup on FP32.

Abstract

Existing attention accelerators often trade exact softmax semantics or suffer from sequential depth that limits throughput. We present ELSA, an algorithmic reformulation of softmax attention that preserves exact semantics in real arithmetic. By casting the online softmax update into a prefix scan over an associative monoid of triples $(m, S, W)$, ELSA achieves $O(n)$ extra memory and $O(\log n)$ parallel depth. Implemented in Triton and CUDA C++, it improves FP32 inference latency over memory-efficient SDPA by 1.3–3.5x as a drop-in replacement with no retraining required.

Method: Parallel Monoid Scan

ELSA transforms the sequential online softmax recurrence into a fully parallelizable computation by proving that attention states $(m, S, W)$ form an associative monoid.

Unlike hardware-fused kernels that depend on HMMA/GMMA instructions, ELSA utilizes a two-level scan architecture (intra-block Hillis-Steele and inter-block Blelloch) that remains hardware-agnostic. This reduces the critical path to $O(\log n)$, providing a high-throughput solution for diverse platforms including older GPUs and embedded systems like Jetson TX2.

Evaluations on NVIDIA A100 show that ELSA's throughput gains scale significantly as sequences grow. It maintains comparable memory footprints to memory-efficient SDPA while delivering massive latency reductions.

Drop-in Acceleration

ELSA is a "Plug-and-Play" solution for pretrained foundation models.

  • CLIP & SAM: Reduces inference time by over 2x for high-resolution images without fine-tuning.
  • Embedded (Jetson TX2): Achieves 1.5x-2.0x lower latency where standard fusion kernels cannot run.
  • Long Context: Sustains stable performance on sequences up to 16K+ tokens in high-precision FP32.
