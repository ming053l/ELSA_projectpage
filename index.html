ELSA: Exact Linear-Scan Attention for Fast and Memory-Light Vision Transformers

Chih-Chung Hsu, Xin-Di Ma, Wo-Ting Liao, Chia-Ming Lee
National Yang Ming Chiao Tung University

CVPR 2026

Paper arXiv Code

ELSA reformulates online attention as a parallel prefix scan, achieving $O(\log n)$ parallel depth while remaining hardware-agnostic and retrain-free.

Abstract

Existing attention accelerators often trade exact softmax semantics, depend on fused Tensor Core kernels, or suffer from sequential depth that limits FP32 throughput on long sequences. We present ELSA, an algorithmic reformulation of softmax attention that:

  • Preserves Exactness: Maintains standard softmax semantics in real arithmetic and provides a provable FP32 relative error bound.
  • Parallelizes Online Softmax: Casts the update into a prefix scan over an associative monoid, yielding $O(n)$ extra memory and $O(\log n)$ parallel depth.
  • Hardware-Agnostic: Independent of Tensor Cores and implemented in Triton and CUDA C++ for high-throughput inference across platforms, from A100 GPUs to Jetson TX2.

ELSA improves FP32 inference latency over memory-efficient SDPA by 1.3–3.5x while remaining a drop-in replacement with no retraining required.
